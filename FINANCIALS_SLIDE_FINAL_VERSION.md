## ‚úÖ FULL VERSION (2 minutes) - Use If You Have Time

### **Slide Title:** "Unit Economics by Tier"

### **What To Say:**

"Here's the unit economics across our three tiers:

**Starter tier‚Äîup to 500 attendees, around 15 booths:**
- Revenue: R2,500  
- Variable cost: R2,120‚Äîlower because fewer attendees, less data, less support time
- Profit: R380  
- Profit margin: 15%
- Use case: Events like AFDA, small campus events

This is our entry point. Slim margin but profitable.

**Professional tier‚Äîup to 2,000 attendees, around 30 booths:**
- Revenue: R8,000  
- Variable cost: R2,858‚Äîmoderate scale, more infrastructure
- Profit: R5,142  
- Profit margin: 64%

This is our target tier. Strong sustainable margins. This is where we make money.

**Enterprise tier‚Äîunlimited attendees, 50+ booths:**
- Revenue: R25,000  
- Variable cost: R4,793‚Äîhigher because more attendees, more data, white-glove support
- Profit: R20,207  
- Profit margin: 81%
- Use case: Large venues like rAge, Sandton Convention Centre

Highest absolute profit per event.

**[Pause]**

Notice the pattern: As events get bigger, our variable costs go up from R2.1k to R2.9k to R4.8k. But revenue goes up FASTER‚Äîfrom R2.5k to R8k to R25k. That's why margins improve from 15% to 64% to 81%. 

We're profitable at every tier, and we get MORE profitable as customers scale. This is classic SaaS economics.

**[Pause]**

In terms of scale: We break even at 23 events per month with a weighted mix of tiers. At 40 events per month, we're at R150,000+ monthly profit.

The key insight: Variable costs scale with event size, but revenue scales faster. Once we cover fixed costs, every new event is 60-80% pure profit."
